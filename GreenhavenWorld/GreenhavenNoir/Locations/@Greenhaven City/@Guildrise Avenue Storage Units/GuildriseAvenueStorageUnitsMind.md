# @Guildrise Avenue Storage Units

@Guildrise Avenue Storage Units is a row of corrugated metal doors on a street
that the city forgot to name twice. The units are rented by the month, cash
only, no questions asked. Unit 227 was rented three months ago by a man who
gave a name that does not exist and an address that burned down twelve years
ago. The lock has just been cut — by the manager, who finally gave up waiting
for payment. What is inside is not furniture. It is a war plan.

## First Entry Bubble

The storage unit's door rolls up with a shriek of rusted metal. The single
bare bulb inside flickers on — and you see them. Maps. Dozens of maps. Metro
maps. Ventilation schematics. Passenger flow charts. A detailed blueprint of
the entire @Greenhaven City underground transit system, with six points circled
in red marker. And one point marked with an X — the central ventilation hub.

On a small crate in the corner: a child's drawing. A crayon picture of a house,
a sun, and three stick figures labelled "Papa, Mama, Lin." The paper is old.
The edges are worn — handled many times. Beneath it: a death certificate. Lina
Voss, age 7. Cause of death: drowning. Location: @Iron Row subsidence event.

This is not just a tactical planning room. This is a memorial.

## Place Canon

@Guildrise Avenue Storage Units is the Wednesday investigation site. Voss
planned the entire attack here. The maps are his tactical blueprint. But the
child's drawing and the death certificate are his HEART — the reason for
everything. The hero learns @Lin's fate here, and begins to understand that
Voss is not a terrorist in the conventional sense. He is a father who declared
war on a machine.

## Sensory Identity

- Color: the grey of corrugated metal, the red of circled points on a map,
  the yellowed paper of a child's drawing, the blue ink of a death
  certificate.
- Sound: the groan of the roll-up door, the buzz of a bare bulb, rain on
  the metal roof, the silence of a room where someone spent months alone
  with a plan.
- Smell: dust, old paper, the faint chemical trace from Voss's clothes
  (the same smell from the lab — sarin residue), crayon wax.
- Texture: the rough edges of worn maps, the smooth metal of the crate,
  the brittle paper of the death certificate.
- Motion: maps curling at the edges from humidity, the bare bulb swaying
  slightly from the opened door, dust motes drifting in the light.

## Points Of Interest

- The Metro Blueprint — a full schematic of the underground. SIX ventilation
  junctions circled in red. Each one is a sarin deployment point. Together,
  they form a closed loop. And the X: the central ventilation hub. Voss's
  position on Sunday night. The hero now knows WHERE.
- The Passenger Flow Chart — peak hours, carriage density, evacuation
  bottlenecks. Sunday evening is highlighted: 8:00 PM to 9:00 PM. Maximum
  passengers, minimum security. Voss chose his moment with surgical precision.
  The hero now knows WHEN.
- The Child's Drawing — a crayon picture. Papa, Mama, Lin. The paper is old
  but undamaged — Voss kept it protected while everything around it gathered
  dust. This is the first piece of the motive: Voss had a family. A daughter.
- The Death Certificate — Lina Voss. Age 7. Cause: drowning. Location:
  @Iron Row subsidence event. Date: three years ago. The subsidence was a
  city infrastructure failure — an unsealed utility tunnel collapsed under
  a street. @Lin fell forty feet into water. The city did not apologise.
  The city cannot apologise. Now the hero understands.
- @Manager Kell — the storage facility manager. Rented Unit 227 to
  a man who called himself "Jan Voss." Paid six months in advance.
  Cash. No questions. Wants the unit cleared by end of day.
- @Voss Locked Strongbox — hidden under a floor panel beneath the
  crate with the child's drawing. Fireproof. Three-digit
  combination. The code is a date. The date is on the death
  certificate. The code is **307** — March 07.

- The Third Map — not of the metro. A map of @Iron Row. The subsidence site
  is circled. Voss drew a line from the site to the metro's central hub. On
  the line, in his handwriting: *"From the place she fell to the place it
  feels."* This is the motive made geography.

## Immediate Player Actions

- Study the metro blueprint. Memorise the six points and the X. The hero
  will need this map on Saturday and Sunday.
- Examine the passenger flow chart. Confirm the timing: Sunday, 8:00-9:00 PM.
- Find the child's drawing. Then find the death certificate. Put them
  together. Understand WHY.
- Trace Voss's line on the third map — from @Iron Row to the central hub.
  The geography of grief.
- Take the maps as evidence — or photograph them. The hero needs the
  tactical information for later in the week.

## Hostile And Rival Pressure

The storage unit is not dangerous. But the knowledge inside it IS. The hero is
now carrying tactical intelligence about a terrorist attack — and the motive
behind it. This changes the investigation from "find the bad guy" to
"understand the bad guy." The moral weight of the case shifts here. Also: the
manager is getting impatient. He wants the unit cleared. The hero has limited
time before the evidence is thrown out.

## Memory And Consequence Hooks

Record: whether the hero found the attack locations and timing (tactical
intelligence), whether the hero found the drawing and the death certificate
(motive), and whether the hero understood the connection between @Lin's death
and the metro hub (Voss's logic).
