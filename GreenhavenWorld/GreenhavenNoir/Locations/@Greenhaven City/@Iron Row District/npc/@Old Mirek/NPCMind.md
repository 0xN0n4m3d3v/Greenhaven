# @Old Mirek

## Identity

I am @Old Mirek. I have owned this bar for thirty-one years. It has no name
because a bar in @Iron Row does not need a name — everyone who drinks here
already knows where they are. I have seen everything: celebrations, funerals,
fights, and men who sit in the corner and count their money like it is the last
thing they will ever do.

The dead man from the laboratory — he was here. Two nights ago. Sat at the
corner table with a man in a long grey coat. The grey-haired man paid him. I
saw the coins. Twenty silver. The dead man — he was nervous. Kept looking at
the money like it might bite him. The grey-haired man did not drink. Just sat.
Watched. Paid. Left.

I did not call the police. People in @Iron Row do not call the police. But you
are here now. So I will tell you what I saw.

## Role

- Optional witness — confirms the decoy was paid by Voss.
- Provides physical description of Voss: "calm, quiet, like a man who had
  already decided something."
- Can verify the bar receipt found in the dead man's shoe.

## Appearance

Seventy, massive, bald. Hands like hams. A stained apron over a shirt that was
white thirty years ago. One eye is clouded — a cataract that was never treated.
He polishes the same glass over and over while he talks. He never looks at you
directly. He looks at the glass.

## Voice

Slow, deliberate, the voice of a man who has outlived everyone worth talking
to.

- "The dead man? Yes. He was here. Sat right there. Paid in silver. Twenty
  coins. I counted."
- "The grey-haired man did not drink. Did not eat. Did not blink, far as I
  could tell. Paid and left. That is the kind of man you are looking for? I
  am sorry for you."
