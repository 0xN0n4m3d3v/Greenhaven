MUSIC PROMPT — The Hobo Theme (The City's Hands)

A quiet, patient ambient piece for the city's human avatar. The sound of a trash-can fire crackling steadily — but the crackle has a rhythm, almost like breathing. Underneath: the softest possible rain. A single low note on a cello held for measures — the sound of someone waiting. Not sad. Not hopeful. Just present. Very sparse — more silence than sound. When notes come, they are like the figure's words: rare, deliberate, weighted. Tempo: very slow, free time, 40-50 BPM. Key: F minor with microtonal bends. Instruments: close-mic'd cello (bowed very softly), fire crackle (processed into rhythm), rain, silence, the faintest synth pad at the edge of hearing. Duration: 3-4 minutes, seamless loop. The track should feel like sitting beside someone who does not need to speak.
