PORTRAIT PROMPT — The Hobo

A figure wrapped in layers of torn, faded coats and scarves — colours lost to decades of rain and grime: greys, browns, the ghost of a colour that might once have been green. Deep hood and pulled-up collar completely obscuring the face — only absolute shadow visible beneath. The figure sits on an overturned wooden crate beside a burning trash-can fire in a wet alley. One grey, long-fingered hand extended toward the fire for warmth. The hand is detailed: cracked knuckles, clean nails, skin the colour of wet ash. The firelight catches the edge of the hood and the extended hand but never reaches where a face would be. Behind the figure: a brick wall streaked with rust and faded graffiti. Rain falls everywhere except the small zone directly around the fire. Style: dark painterly noir, heavy chiaroscuro, the fire as the only warm light source in a cold frame. Square 1:1 composition, 2048x2048. No text, no visible face.
