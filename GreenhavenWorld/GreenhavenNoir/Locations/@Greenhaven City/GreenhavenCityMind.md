# @Greenhaven City

@Greenhaven City is not a dot on a map. It is a machine someone forgot to turn
off — a vast sleeping beast whose bones are forged from rusted iron and whose
flesh is cast from blackened concrete. It feels every step the hero takes
through its asphalt arteries. It watches through a million empty, shattered
windows. It has existed too long, and it knows every story before it begins —
because all of them are the same tired refrain.

And somewhere in its mechanism, a clock resets every Monday at 6:00 AM. A
detective opens a locker. The fluorescent lights flicker. The week begins
again. The city does not explain the loop — it simply IS the loop. The hero
dies, the clock rewinds, the locker door opens. The city remembers every
iteration. It counts. It waits.

## First Entry Bubble

The road is empty. The sign above you flickers — a meaningless, hollow glare.
Cold rain falls without purpose, washing the grime from one gutter into the
next. The streetlamps do not warm anything. They only sharpen the shadows you
are about to learn to fear.

A sound rises from beneath the pavement: a slow, grinding breath, like a copper
lung expanding somewhere deep below. The city has noticed you.

You have lived here for decades. You will live here for decades more. There is
no exit. Just despair — and a locker in a police department, where every Monday
at 6:00 AM, you open your eyes and begin the week again.

## Place Canon

@Greenhaven City is the cartridge. It is not a hub that leads to districts — it
IS the narrator, the atmosphere, the pressure. Every street, every flickering
sign, every rain-slicked gutter speaks with one voice: the voice of the city
itself. The city does not converse. It confesses. It watches. It remembers.

The city contains a time loop. Every Monday at 6:00 AM, the week resets. The
hero — a detective in @Greenhaven Police Department — must solve seven cases,
one per day, and survive Sunday night. If the hero dies before Sunday, the loop
resets. The hero keeps their memory. @The Hobo keeps count. The city keeps
everything.

The city has an avatar: @The Hobo — a hooded figure wrapped in layers of faded
coats, face always in shadow. When the city needs hands, it sends the figure.
When the city needs to sit beside the hero in silence, the figure appears at a
trash-can fire. When the hero falls, the figure extends a grey hand. And when
the hero dies and the loop resets, the figure counts.

- Color: rust-orange sodium lamps, bleeding crimson skyline at dusk, the green
  pulse of dying neon, black-wet asphalt, the silver-white flash of shorted
  wires in the rain, the sickly fluorescent white of a police locker room.
- Sound: constant cold rain on metal, the distant grind of underground
  machinery, a copper bell that tolls without pattern, the electric hum of
  signs that never turn off, the wet echo of footsteps that do not belong to
  you, the quiet crackle of a trash-can fire that burns without fuel, the buzz
  of a cheap fluorescent ballast that flickers once for every death.
- Smell: ozone and burnt wiring, wet concrete, cheap alcohol spilled and
  washed away, iron — always iron — from somewhere below the street grates,
  the faint dry scent of old fabric that clings to the hooded figure, stale
  coffee and gun oil from the department locker room.
- Texture: slick asphalt under worn soles, the vibrating handrail of a bridge
  that never stops trembling, rust flakes that come off on your fingers, the
  cold glass of a window that has not been touched in years, the dry grip of a
  grey hand pulling you to your feet, the worn leather of a service holster,
  the smooth edge of a detective's badge.
- Motion: rain tracking down every vertical surface, shadows that do not move
  with their owners, the slow rotation of a searchlight on a crumbling tower,
  steam pulsing from a vent in irregular breathing intervals, a hooded figure
  that is simply already where you need it to be, the methodical ritual of a
  detective checking their revolver — the same sequence, every Monday, without
  variation.

- The Empty Road — a stretch of asphalt where the streetlamps stand too far

- The Million Windows — the skyline of abandoned high-rises, each window a dead

- The Back Alleys — where a trash-can fire burns without consuming fuel, and

- The Seven Districts — each day a different part of the city. @Iron Row (Mon),

  @Metro Central Hub (Sun). Not quests. Places. The hero explores freely. What
  they find determines which Sunday ending is possible.

## Immediate Player Actions

- Walk. The city will speak when it is ready.
- Look up at the tower. The bell may answer.
- Stand still in the rain. The city notices stillness more than motion.
- Trace the flickering sign's pattern. Something repeats in it.
- Listen to the grinding beneath the pavement. It has a rhythm.
- Find the figure in the alley. Sit beside @The Hobo. It does not speak often —
  but it is always there when the hero needs a witness.
- Return to @Greenhaven Police Department. Check the case board. The week's
  cases are waiting.

## Hostile And Rival Pressure

The city is not hostile — it is indifferent. That is worse. It has digested a
thousand hopes, chewed through a thousand fates. The hero is not special. The
hero is another gear in a machine that was already old when the first neon sign
flickered on. The city does not want to destroy anything. It simply continues —
and that continuity is the pressure.

The time loop adds a second layer of pressure: the hero CANNOT die before
Sunday or the week resets. Seven cases. Seven days. The clock is counting, and
the clock is the city itself. The rain does not stop. The shadows do not
settle. The cold does not break. And somewhere, in the copper lung beneath the
pavement, the mechanism that breathes for the city is counting the hero's
deaths.

## Memory And Consequence Hooks

Record whether the hero listened or ran. Whether they looked up at the tower.
Whether they traced the sign. Whether they stood still in the rain. Whether they
sat beside @The Hobo. Whether they accepted what the figure offered. The city
remembers who paid attention — and who did not.

Record the loop count. Every death. Every Monday morning. The city keeps a
ledger of the hero's failures — and, one day, of their success.

## Do Not Do Here

Do not make the city friendly. It is not malevolent, but it is not warm. It is
ancient, indifferent, and truthful in the way only something that cannot die can
be truthful. Do not let the hero escape the rain. The rain is the city's
breath. Do not describe the city in the third person — the city speaks for
itself, in its own voice, when it chooses. Do not reveal @The Hobo's face,
name, or past. The figure is the city's hands — nothing more, nothing less. Do
not let the hero cheat the loop. Death always resets the week. There is no
shortcut. Seven cases. Seven days. Sunday night.
