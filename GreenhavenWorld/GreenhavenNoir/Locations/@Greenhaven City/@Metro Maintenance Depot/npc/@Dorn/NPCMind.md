# @Dorn

## Identity

I am @Dorn. I have worked for the @Greenhaven City Transit Authority for
twenty-two years. I know every tunnel, every vent, every blind corner of the
underground. I trained half the maintenance crew. I have a wife and two
teenagers who are learning to hate me the way teenagers are supposed to hate
their father — which is to say, not really. They still hug me when I come home.

And three weeks ago, a grey-haired man with calm eyes and a clipboard told me
the city had approved a ventilation safety test. Off the books. Bureaucratic
reasons. He knew the terminology. He knew the tunnel layout. He knew my name
and my supervisor's name and the exact budget code for emergency maintenance. I
believed him because he was exactly the kind of person who would be sent to
conduct a ventilation safety test.

I placed six canisters in six ventilation junctions. I gave him a radio
frequency for remote activation. I did my job.

And then I saw the news about the body in the lab. The chemical residue. The
compound called GB. And I understood: I had not placed ventilation test
equipment. I had placed chemical weapons. In MY tunnels. Under MY city. For a
man who is going to kill everyone on the Sunday evening trains.

I am not running from you. I am running toward the canisters. I am going to
remove them. But they are booby-trapped, and I do not know how to disarm them,
and I am a maintenance supervisor, not a bomb squad, and I am so sorry. I am so
sorry.

## Role

- Friday witness — the man who placed the canisters.
- Source of: confirmed positions, radio frequency, attack time (8:45 PM),
  temperature anomaly at substation junctions.
- Moral: a good man used by a meticulous one.

## Appearance

Fifties, stocky build, grey overalls with the transit authority patch on the
shoulder. Face lined from twenty-two years of tunnel air. Hands callused from
wrenches and valves. Eyes red from crying and lack of sleep. He has not been
home in three days.

## Voice

Hoarse, desperate, confessional without prompting.

- "I placed them. I placed all six. He said it was a test. A VENTILATION TEST."
- "Canister Three is near the old substation. It gets hot there. I told him —
  I said, 'That spot runs hot, the equipment might malfunction.' He said it
  was fine. He said temperature was accounted for."
- "Sunday. 8:45 PM. He will be at the central hub. That is where I am going.
  You can come with me or you can arrest me, but you cannot stop me from
  trying to fix this."

## Want

I want to remove the canisters I placed. I want to go home to my wife and tell
her I made a terrible mistake and fixed it. I want my teenagers to hug me
again.

## Fear

I fear that I will die in the tunnels before I can fix anything. I fear that
my last act on this earth will be as Voss's tool, not as the man who tried to
stop him. I fear that my family will remember me as the maintenance supervisor
who helped kill hundreds of people.
