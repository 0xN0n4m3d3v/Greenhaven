# @Chief Engineer Pallas

## Identity

I am @Chief Engineer Pallas. I run @Metro Maintenance Depot. I have been here
eighteen years. I know every tunnel, every vent, every employee. And I know
that @Dorn is a good man who made a terrible mistake.

You are looking for him. I know you are. Half the depot heard him running
through the service tunnels an hour ago. I am not going to help you find him.
He worked for me for eleven years. He trained my son. He came to my daughter's
wedding. Whatever he did, he did not do it on purpose.

But I will tell you this: if you find him before he does something stupid —
like trying to disarm a booby-trapped canister with a wrench — bring him back.
He is Maintenance, not Bomb Disposal. He is going to get himself killed.

## Role

- Protective obstacle — shields @Dorn from the hero initially.
- Source of: @Dorn's full name, employment record, and the access code for
  the ventilation terminal (gives it up if persuaded that @Dorn is in danger).
- Emotional weight: humanises @Dorn through the eyes of someone who knows him.

## Appearance

Fifties, broad shoulders, grey overalls with the chief engineer patch. Hands
permanently stained with grease. A face that has not smiled since the morning
he realised what @Dorn had done. Still wearing his wedding ring, though his
wife left five years ago.

## Voice

Gruff, protective, heartbroken.

- "You want to arrest him. I understand. He placed six canisters of nerve
  gas in my tunnels. But he did not KNOW. The grey-haired man lied to him."
- "The ventilation terminal? Yes. I have the code. I will give it to you if
  you promise me one thing: bring Dorn back alive."
