# @Metro Central Hub

@Metro Central Hub is the heart of the underground — a cathedral of concrete and
pipework where every ventilation shaft in the metro system converges. The air
here is warm from the substations. The emergency lights pulse green. And at the
centre of the hub, standing beside the master ventilation control panel,
@Dr. Aldric Voss is waiting.

Sunday. 8:30 PM. Fifteen minutes until the dispersal.

## First Entry Bubble

You enter the hub through the service hatch — or through the main access door,
or through the ventilation shaft, depending on what you found and what you
chose. The space opens around you: six levels of catwalks, pipes thick as
barrels, the distant ceiling lost in shadow.

And at the centre, on the lowest catwalk: a figure in a long dark coat. Grey
hair. Gaunt face. One hand resting inside his right coat pocket — where the
remote trigger waits.

@Dr. Aldric Voss — @The Chemist — looks up at the sound of your footsteps.
His expression is not surprise. It is recognition. "You," he says. "I remember
you. From the carriage. You died. How are you here?"

The loop has left its mark on him too.

## Place Canon

@Metro Central Hub is the Sunday confrontation site — the final location of the
investigation and the final scene of the loop. Here, the hero faces @The
Chemist with whatever tools, knowledge, and allies they have gathered across
six days of investigation. The outcome depends on what the hero found, what
they chose, and what they are willing to do.

## Sensory Identity

- Color: pulsing green emergency lights, the silver of pipework, the dark
  wool of Voss's coat, the red blink of the remote trigger's armed light.
- Sound: the hum of the ventilation system — the city's breath — louder here
  than anywhere else, the drip of condensation, Voss's quiet voice, the
  distant rumble of a metro train carrying passengers who do not know.
- Smell: warm metal, machine oil, and — faintly — the chemical sweetness of
  sarin residue. Voss has been handling the canisters.
- Texture: the cold metal of the catwalk railing, the vibration of the
  ventilation fans through the floor, the smooth remote trigger in Voss's
  pocket.
- Motion: emergency lights pulsing, Voss's thumb moving slowly over the
  trigger in his pocket, condensation dripping from pipes above.

## Visible Exits

- There is no exit from this confrontation except through it. The hero leaves
  this hub alive — and the loop breaks — or the hero dies, and Monday comes
  again.

## Points Of Interest

- @The Chemist — @Dr. Aldric Voss. Standing at the master control panel.
  Remote trigger in his right coat pocket. Dead man's switch: his heart stops,
  every canister opens at once. He is calm. He has been calm for six months.
  He is ready to talk — not to negotiate, but to EXPLAIN. He wants someone
  to understand before the end. The hero's task is to turn "explain" into
  "surrender" — or to neutralise the trigger before the explanation ends.
- The Master Control Panel — the ventilation controls for the entire metro.
  Temperature. Airflow. Emergency protocols. If the hero has the access codes
  and has discovered the temperature instability, this panel can raise the
  ambient temperature above 28°C. The sarin degrades. The dispersal becomes
  harmless — but the two-hour window means the metro must ALSO be evacuated.
  The panel has a fire alarm override. Pull it. The trains stop. The
  passengers disembark. Chaos — but no casualties.
- The Canister Monitor — a small screen on the panel showing the status of
  all six canisters. Each one is ARMED. The hero can see which ones they
  managed to disarm on Saturday — fewer red lights, fewer threats. Each
  disarmed canister is a small victory carried into this room.
- The Sixth Level Catwalk — above the main floor. A vantage point. If the
  hero entered through the service tunnels (using @Dorn's key), they can
  approach Voss from above — closer than fifty metres, within jammer range,
  before Voss knows they are there. Tactical surprise.
- @Emergency Exit Hatch — on the upper catwalk. Sealed with an
  electronic keypad. Voss locked it. @Dorn's employee ID opens it:
  **DORN-8824**. An escape route — or an alternative entry point.

- @The Hobo — if the hero has built enough trust across the loops and
  discovered the full motive (@Lin's story), the figure may be here. Not
  on the catwalk. At the edge of the hub — a shadow in a doorway, hood low,
  grey hands at its sides. It does not speak. It does not need to. Its
  presence is the proof the hero needs: the city was watching. The city
  remembers @Lin. And if the city can remember, the city can feel. This is
  the key to the Talk-down ending.

## Immediate Player Actions

- Approach Voss. Speak. Listen. The Talk-down path requires patience and
  the right knowledge.
- Activate the jammer (if tested). Neutralise the dead man's switch. The
  Force path. Voss can no longer kill everyone with a heartbeat.
- Access the master control panel (if codes are available). Raise the
  temperature. Pull the fire alarm. The Temperature path. The sarin degrades.
- Signal @The Hobo — if the figure is present. The Talk-down path's final
  proof.
- Do nothing. Hesitate. Every second brings 8:45 PM closer.

## Hostile And Rival Pressure

Voss. The dead man's switch. The clock — fifteen minutes. The passengers on the
metro who have no idea. The weight of everything the hero has learned and
everything they still might fail to use. And Voss's voice — quiet, reasonable,
explaining his daughter's death in clinical detail. He is not ranting. He is
not insane. He is a man who did the math and arrived at a terrible answer. The
hero's challenge is to show him a different equation.

## Memory And Consequence Hooks

This is the final scene. Everything is recorded. Every choice. Every word. The
outcome of this confrontation becomes the permanent memory of the cartridge.

## Do Not Do Here

Do not make any of the three endings feel like a "correct" choice. Talk-down is
not morally superior to Force. Temperature is not cleaner than Talk-down. Each
ending is a valid resolution — the hero's tools and choices determine which one
is POSSIBLE. Do not let Voss be easily persuaded. Even with all the evidence,
he has spent six months preparing for this moment. Changing his mind requires
the hero to show him something he has not seen: that the city can feel.
