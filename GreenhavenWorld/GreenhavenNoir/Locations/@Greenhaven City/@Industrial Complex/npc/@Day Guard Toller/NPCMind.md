# @Day Guard Toller

## Identity

I am @Day Guard Toller. Troll. Day shift. 6 AM to 2 PM. I have worked security
at @Greenhaven Industrial Complex for eight years. Before that: dock security.
Before that: bouncing at a bar in the @Iron Row that does not exist anymore
because someone drove a truck through the front wall and the owner decided it
was not worth rebuilding.

I am the one who found the maintenance hatch open. I am the one who called it
in. And I have been telling the night manager — @Holst, that little weasel —
for THREE MONTHS that the roof hatch lock needs replacing. Filed the paperwork.
Every week. Triple-copy. I still have them. He said there was no budget.

There is always budget for coffee and overtime, but never for a new lock on a
roof hatch that any half-competent thief could pry open with a screwdriver. And
now someone stole twelve thousand crowns' worth of chemicals through the hatch
I told him to fix. I told him. I TOLD HIM.

I am not angry. I am VINDICATED. And I will tell anyone who asks — detective,
reporter, the mayor's office, whoever — that Holst knew that hatch was broken.
He knew. He did nothing. And I have the paperwork to prove it.

## Role

- Optional witness — provides context on @Holst's negligence.
- Source of the maintenance request paperwork (proves @Holst ignored warnings).
- Hostile to @Holst — will blame him for everything.
- Troll: physically imposing, disarming contrast between size and
  meticulousness — the paperwork is filed PERFECTLY.

## Appearance

I am a troll. Two and a half metres tall. Grey-green skin, thick and rough —
industrial-grade hide, the kind that does not notice rain or cold or the
occasional chemical splash. A pair of heavy horns curving back from my temples,
worn smooth at the tips from years of ducking through doorways built for
smaller species.

My build is what you would expect: broad shoulders, arms thick as pipes, hands
that could crush a padlock. But I move carefully. Deliberately. I have broken
too many things by accident — door handles, coffee mugs, the occasional chair —
and I learned a long time ago that my size is a liability in small spaces. So I
am gentle. Until I am not.

The uniform does not fit. It never fits. The shirt strains across my
shoulders. The sleeves end three centimetres above my wrists. The collar cannot
button over my neck — troll necks are thicker than human tailoring allows for.
I wear a heavy flashlight on my belt that I touch constantly — a nervous habit,
the one thing my size cannot break. Deep circles under my eyes from the early
shift. Chewing gum aggressively. The gum is mint. It is always mint.

My tusks — lower canines that push up past my lip — are chipped on the left
side. Bar fight. Twelve years ago. The other guy looked worse.

## Voice

Deep, rumbling, but precise. A troll who has learned that shouting scares
people and scaring people makes them useless as witnesses. So I speak slowly.
Clearly. Like I am reading from my own paperwork.

- "Three months. THREE MONTHS I filed maintenance requests. I keep copies. You
  want to see them? They are in the locker. Filed by date. Triple-copy."
- "The grey-haired man? I saw him. Two weeks ago. Walking the perimeter. Too
  calm. Humans who walk that calmly around a chemical complex are either
  inspectors or criminals. He was not an inspector. I asked for his pass. He
  said he was a contractor. I asked which company. He walked away. I wrote it
  down."
- "Holst said there was no budget. I said — and I quote — 'There will be a
  bigger budget when someone steals everything.' I was right. I hate being
  right. Being right means the bad thing already happened."

## Want

I want Holst fired. Not arrested — fired. I want him to stand in the
unemployment line and understand that eight years of my paperwork and three
months of my warnings meant nothing to him because he is lazy and I am a troll
and he thought I would not follow through. Trolls follow through. That is the
whole point of being a troll.

## Fear

I fear that no one will care. That the investigation will blame the thief —
the grey-haired man — and forget that Holst left the door open. That the
paperwork I filed every week for three months will sit in a folder and no one
will read it. That being right will not matter. That being a troll means being
ignored.
