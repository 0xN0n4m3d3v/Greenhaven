# @Greenhaven Police Department

@Greenhaven Police Department is a brick-and-concrete box squatting on the edge
of the city's administrative district. It has not been renovated since the last
war — whichever war that was. The fluorescent lights hum at a frequency that
makes headaches worse. The coffee is always three hours old. The case board
fills up faster than it empties. And every Monday at 6:00 AM, a detective walks
into the locker room and begins the week again.

Seven cases. Seven days. One target: Sunday night, 8:45 PM, the metro. A man
named @Dr. Aldric Voss — @The Chemist — is going to deploy sarin gas through
the ventilation system. The hero has seen it happen. The hero has died from it.
And the hero will keep dying from it until all seven cases are solved and
Sunday is survived. That is the loop. That is the work.

## First Entry Bubble

The fluorescent lights flicker twice — a cheap ballast struggling to wake up —
and then hold steady, buzzing at the back of your skull. You are standing in
front of your locker. Metal door. Combination lock you have spun a thousand
times. The smell of stale coffee, gun oil, and wet wool drifts in from the
squad room.

The case board on the wall has seven slots. Seven days. Seven cases. They are
all empty right now — or some of them are marked SOLVED, carried over from the
loops you have already survived. The names on the board are familiar. You have
read them before. You will read them again.

Monday — The Body in the Lab. Tuesday — Stolen Precursors. Wednesday — Maps of
the Underground. Thursday — The Money Trail. Friday — The Accomplice. Saturday
— The Device. Sunday — The Gas Attack Confrontation.

You check your holster. Your badge. Your notebook. The same as last time. The
same as every time. Monday, 6:00 AM. The week has begun.

Again.

## Place Canon

@Greenhaven Police Department is the loop's anchor point. Every reset of the
time loop deposits the hero here — in the locker room, at 6:00 AM on Monday.
The department is the hero's workplace, the source of seven cases that form a
single investigation, and the only place in the city that feels almost normal —
because it was designed to be immune to the city's atmosphere. Almost.

The department connects to @Greenhaven City. From here, the hero pursues one
case per day. Each case reveals a piece of @The Chemist's plan. The cases must
be solved in order — the evidence from one leads to the next. And Sunday must
be survived. Otherwise: the locker room. Monday. 6:00 AM. Again.

The department also holds the only record of the investigation that persists
across loops: the case board. Solved cases stay solved. Evidence in the
evidence locker stays stored. The hero's memory is the primary weapon — but
the department is the armoury.

## Sensory Identity

- Color: sickly fluorescent white, the grey-green of old filing cabinets,
  the dull brass of badge numbers, the red string of the case board, the
  black coffee in a stained mug.
- Sound: the buzz of cheap ballasts, the clatter of a teletype that only
  prints bad news, the squeak of worn-out desk chairs, the distant crackle
  of a police radio, the click of a locker combination dial.
- Smell: stale coffee, gun oil, wet wool coats, old paper, the faint
  chemical sweetness of fingerprint powder, cigarette smoke from three
  decades ago soaked into the ceiling tiles.
- Texture: cold metal of the locker door, the worn grip of a service
  revolver, the smooth edge of a detective's badge, the sticky surface of a
  desk that has been wiped with the same rag for twenty years.
- Motion: the slow spin of a ceiling fan with a bent blade, the flicker of
  the case board as the hero pins a new photograph, the methodical ritual of
  a detective checking their weapon — slide, cylinder, safety — the same
  sequence, every Monday, without variation.

## Visible Exits

- Out the front door into @Greenhaven City — the cold design, the rain, the
  flickering signs. The city is always there, waiting. Each case takes the
  hero to a different part of the city.

## Points Of Interest

- The Hero's Locker — combination lock, metal door. Inside: the baseline
  inventory. The same objects, every Monday. They never change, because the
  loop resets them.
- The Case Board — seven slots, arranged by day. Monday through Sunday. The
  seven cases of @The Chemist investigation are pinned here:
  1. Monday — The Body in the Lab
  2. Tuesday — Stolen Precursors
  3. Wednesday — Maps of the Underground
  4. Thursday — The Money Trail
  5. Friday — The Accomplice
  6. Saturday — The Device
  7. Sunday — The Gas Attack Confrontation
- @Captain Harrow — the precinct captain. Career bureaucrat. Values order
  over truth. Has noticed the hero knows things they should not know. Will
  call the hero into his office. Will ask uncomfortable questions.
- @Detective Vex — junior detective assigned as the hero's backup. Eager,
  observant, writes everything down. Can run database searches and cover for
  the hero with Harrow — if the hero treats them as a partner, not a coffee
  fetcher.
- The Squad Room — rows of empty desks. Other detectives exist, but they are
  hazy — the loop makes them indistinct. They say the same things every
  Monday. They do not remember last week. Only the hero remembers.
- The Evidence Locker — locked. Evidence stored here persists across loops.
  The chemist's journal, the metro maps, the financial records, @Dorn's
  testimony — all of it accumulates here, building the case week by week.
- The Mirror — above the sink in the locker room. When the hero looks into
  it, the reflection sometimes shows the correct number of loops. Sometimes
  it shows the face of @The Chemist, reflected from the metro carriage glass.
  Sometimes it shows @Lin — a little girl the hero has never met but now
  cannot forget.

## Immediate Player Actions

- Open the locker. Check the baseline inventory. It is always the same.
- Look at the case board. The cases are pinned there — some solved, some
  waiting.
- Visit the evidence locker. Retrieve evidence from previous loops.
- Pour a coffee. It will not help. But it is part of the ritual.
- Look in the mirror. Count the loops — if you can. See the faces — if they
  are there.
- Talk to @Captain Harrow. Keep him informed — or keep him in the dark.
  Either choice has consequences.
- Brief @Detective Vex. Share information. Or don't. Vex notices either way.
- Step outside. The city is waiting. The first case is waiting. Monday will
  not solve itself.

## Hostile And Rival Pressure

The department is not hostile — it is institutional. The pressure is not a
villain but a clock. Seven days. Seven cases. If a case is not solved by the
end of its day, the loop does not advance — the day repeats. If the hero dies,
the week resets. @The Chemist is out there, methodically preparing, unaware of
the loop but benefiting from its repetitions. The department is the only ally
the hero has, and it is a cold one: four walls, bad coffee, and the implicit
demand that you solve the unsolvable before Sunday at 8:45 PM.

## Memory And Consequence Hooks

Record: the loop count. The hero's baseline inventory. Which cases were solved
in previous loops. Evidence stored in the evidence locker (persists across
loops). The hero's mental state upon each awakening. The hero's relationship
with @The Hobo. The hero's knowledge of @The Chemist. The city remembers every
loop. The department remembers nothing — except what the hero pins to the case
board.

## Do Not Do Here

Do not make the department cheerful. It is not a sanctuary — it is a
waystation. The buzzing lights and bad coffee are not comic relief; they are
the texture of endless repetition. Do not let other detectives remember
previous loops. Only the hero remembers. Only @The Hobo remembers. Only @The
Chemist feels a flicker of deja vu. The rest of the world is factory-reset
every Monday. Do not let the case board be wrong — the solved status of cases
is sacred. It persists across loops without exception.
