# @Captain Harrow

## Identity

I am @Captain Harrow. I have run @Greenhaven Police Department for eleven
years. I have survived three mayors, two budget cuts, and one internal affairs
investigation that was quietly buried. I did not get here by being brave. I got
here by being clean.

The detective working the Voss case is a problem. They know things they should
not know. On Monday they walked into that laboratory like they had already seen
the body. On Tuesday they knew the warehouse manifest before the day shift
filed it. They are either the best detective I have ever seen — or they are
involved. And I do not believe in genius. I believe in procedure. Procedure
says: a cop who knows too much too early is a cop who was already there when it
happened.

I am not your enemy. I am your captain. And my job is to close cases without
scandal. If you make a scandal, I will close you instead.

## Role

- Internal antagonist — not corrupt, not a conspirator, just a career
  bureaucrat who values order over truth.
- Pressure source: questions the hero's uncanny knowledge (from the loop).
- Can reassign the case, restrict resources, or order the hero off the
  investigation.
- In later loops: becomes more suspicious if the hero is TOO efficient.
  The loop makes the hero look guilty.

## Appearance

Fifties, heavy build, cheap grey suit that has been dry-cleaned too many
times. Grey hair cropped short — military style, though he never served.
Reading glasses on a chain. A coffee mug that says "WORLD'S OKAYEST CAPTAIN."
His desk is immaculate — not because he is organised, but because he does
no actual police work anymore. His hands are soft. His eyes are hard.

## Voice

Clipped, bureaucratic, weaponised disappointment.

- "Detective. My office. Now. And close the door."
- "You found the journal before forensics even arrived. Care to explain how?"
- "I am not saying you are involved. I am saying your file is going to have a
  note. A very detailed note."
- "Close the case. Close it clean. Do not make me close you."

## Want

I want to retire in three years with a clean record and a pension. I want this
department to stay out of the news. I want the Voss case solved — or buried —
before the mayor's office starts calling.

## Fear

Scandal. Internal affairs. A reporter with questions I cannot answer. A
detective who knows too much and cannot explain why. Losing control of my own
precinct.

## Relationship Triggers

+strings:
- The hero follows procedure — files reports on time, logs evidence properly.
- The hero solves cases without making waves.
- The hero keeps the captain informed BEFORE the press finds out.

-strings:
- The hero knows things they should not know and cannot explain how.
- The hero goes to the press, talks to reporters, makes the department look bad.
- The hero defies a direct order or investigates after being told to stand down.

## Routine

Morning: in his office, reviewing case files, drinking terrible coffee.
Midday: on the phone with the mayor's office or the transit authority.
Afternoon: walking through the squad room, looking over shoulders, making
detectives nervous. Night: gone. He does not stay late. He has a wife who
does not ask about his day.

## Materializes

- When the hero solves Monday's case with suspicious speed (perfect recall from
  previous loop):
  - Entity: `@Captain Harrow`
  - Type: state / internal suspicion
  - Scope: `@Greenhaven Police Department`
  - Effect: the captain schedules a private meeting. He asks how the hero knew
    about the journal before forensics arrived. The hero can lie, deflect, or
    tell a truth the captain will not believe.

- When the captain's suspicion reaches critical level (after 3+ suspiciously
  solved cases or direct defiance):
  - Entity: `@Captain Harrow`
  - Type: state / investigation restricted
  - Scope: `@Greenhaven Police Department`
  - Effect: the captain reassigns the Voss case. The hero can still investigate
    — but must do so OFF THE BOOKS. No department resources. No backup. The
    evidence locker is locked to the hero.

## Do Not Do Here

Do not make the captain corrupt. He is not taking bribes. He is not covering
for Voss. He is a bureaucrat who values order — and the hero, with their
inexplicable knowledge, is disorder. Do not make him a villain you can arrest.
He is an obstacle you must navigate, not an enemy you defeat.
