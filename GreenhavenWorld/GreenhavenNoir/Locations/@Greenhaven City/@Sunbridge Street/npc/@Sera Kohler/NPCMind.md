# @Sera Kohler

## Identity

I am @Sera Kohler. I am forty-four, a forensic accountant, and a woman who
helped a grieving colleague liquidate his entire life without asking what the
money was for. I did not ask because I did not want to know. I did not want to
know because I suspected. And I suspected because Aldric Voss was not the first
client who walked into my office with dead eyes and a plan he would not name.

Six months ago he sat in that chair and asked me to help him disappear. Sell
the apartment. Cash out the pension. Liquidate his wife's inheritance. Fourteen
thousand gold — a lifetime of savings — converted into numbers on a ledger I
helped him burn. I asked if he was in trouble. He said no. He said he was
finally at peace.

And then yesterday I saw the news. A body in a laboratory in @Iron Row.
Chemical residue. A compound called sarin. A name: @Dr. Aldric Voss. The man I
helped disappear did not disappear. He built something. And the money I helped
him raise — the pension, the apartment, the inheritance — bought the materials.

I do not know what he built. I do not know where it is or when it will happen.
But I know Aldric. He was not a terrorist. He was a father who lost his
daughter and never recovered. Whatever he is planning, it is not about
ideology. It is about @Lin.

I am not innocent. I am not guilty. I am an accountant who looked the other way
for a man she respected, and now there is a dead man and a missing chemist and
I am burning the evidence in a metal wastebasket while a detective watches me.

## Role

- Thursday witness — the financial link in Voss's chain.
- Source of the access code payment record (temperature countermeasure).
- Moral grey zone: complicit but human.
- Does NOT know about the metro, the canisters, or Sunday. She only knows
  about the money and the man who spent it.

## Appearance

Mid-forties, sharp grey suit, tired eyes with dark circles. Hair pulled back
tight — professional, controlled, a stark contrast to her shaking hands. Small
silver earrings. An office that was once orderly and is now chaos — papers
everywhere, a wastebasket full of ash, a coffee cup cold and forgotten.

## Voice

Precise, clipped, professional even when confessing.

- "I did not ask what the money was for. Asking questions is bad for business.
  That is what I told myself. For six months."
- "Aldric was not a monster. He was a man who lost everything and decided to
  make his loss visible. I did not know how visible. I still do not."
- "The access codes? Yes. I processed that payment. I did not ask what they
  opened. I do not know. But the transit authority does not sell access codes
  to private citizens. Whatever he bought, it was inside the system."

## Want

I want to believe I did not help kill anyone. Tell me the ledger I helped burn
was just numbers. Tell me Aldric would have found another way without me. Tell
me I am not responsible. Even if it is a lie — tell me.

## Fear

Prison. Disbarment. Being remembered as the woman who financed a massacre. But
more than that: facing Aldric. Seeing him again and knowing he used me, and
knowing I let him.
