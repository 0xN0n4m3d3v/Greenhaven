# @Sunbridge Street

@Sunbridge Street is a narrow lane of failing businesses above which people
live, work, and hide. A laundromat. A pawnshop. A bar with no sign. And on the
third floor of the building above the laundromat: the office of @Sera Kohler,
forensic accountant — a woman who helped a grieving colleague liquidate his
entire life and asked no questions. She is burning documents in a metal
wastebasket when you arrive.

## First Entry Bubble

The smell of burning paper leads you up three flights of stairs. The door to
Office 3C is open. @Sera Kohler — mid-forties, sharp grey suit, tired eyes —
is standing over a metal wastebasket, feeding pages into the flames. She looks
up when you enter. She does not look surprised.

"I wondered when someone would come." She holds up a half-burned ledger page.
"Aldric Voss. Seventeen years at the complex. Three published papers. A wife.
A daughter. And six months ago, he walked into my office and asked me to help
him disappear." She drops the page into the fire. "I did not ask what the money
was for. I am an accountant. Asking questions is bad for business."

She gestures at a filing cabinet. "Everything that is left is in there. The
rest is ash. You have about ten minutes before I finish burning it."

## Place Canon

@Sunbridge Street is the Thursday investigation site. @Sera Kohler is Voss's
former colleague and the person who helped him liquidate his assets. Through
her, the hero learns: Voss spent everything. Fourteen thousand @Gold coin. His
pension. His apartment. His wife's inheritance. Every coin converted into
chemical precursors, equipment, payoffs, and six custom-built sarin dispersal
canisters. He has no escape plan because he has no future to escape to.

@Sera also unknowingly processed a payment for metro ventilation access codes.
Those codes control the ambient temperature in the underground. And temperature
is the key to neutralising sarin.

- Smell: burning paper, old ink, coffee, the faint lavender of @Sera's

- Texture: the crisp edge of a half-burned ledger page, the cold metal of

## Points Of Interest

- @Sera Kohler — mid-forties, accountant, complicit but not evil. She helped
  a grieving colleague and did not ask questions. She is burning the evidence
  not to protect Voss but to protect HERSELF — her career, her licence, her
  freedom. Under pressure, she cooperates. She wants to believe she did not
  help kill anyone. The hero can decide whether to give her that absolution.
- The Filing Cabinet — what @Sera has not yet burned. Contains: Voss's
  financial records, the liquidation documents, a list of every purchase he
  made in the last six months. Every item is dated. Every item leads to
  Sunday.
- The Access Code Payment — buried in the ledger: a payment to a metro
  maintenance supervisor for "ventilation system access codes — full
  clearance." The supervisor is not named in the ledger, but the payment
  went through a credit union that serves metro employees. The hero can
  trace it. This is the THURSDAY CRITICAL FIND — the temperature
  countermeasure path.
- The Last Entry — on @Sera's desk, not yet burned: a handwritten note from
  Voss. *"Sera — thank you. Do not blame yourself. You did not know. No one
  knew. I made sure of that. — A."* The note is dated three days ago. Saturday.
  The day before the first gas attack.
- @Client Bren — an importer of industrial mops, waiting in the
  hallway for @Sera to do his quarterly taxes. Entirely innocent.
  Deeply annoyed. Saw a grey-haired man here last week.
- @Sera Encrypted Laptop — on the desk. Password-protected. The
  hint is on a sticky note: "Q3 closeout date." The date is on
  the wall calendar. The password is **0930** — September 30.

- The Window — @Sera's office window looks out over @Sunbridge Street. In the
  distance: the Crumbling Tower. If the hero stands at this window, @The Hobo
  may be visible below — a hooded figure at the street corner, looking up. Not
  interfering. Just witnessing.

## Immediate Player Actions

- Stop @Sera from burning more documents. Get to the filing cabinet before
  everything is ash.
- Question @Sera about Voss's state of mind. She saw him six months ago and
  once since — he was calm. Too calm. "Like a man who had already decided."
- Find the access code payment in the ledger. Trace it to the metro credit
  union. This is the temperature path.
- Read Voss's note. The gratitude. The isolation. He protected everyone
  who helped him by telling them nothing.
- Look out the window. Find @The Hobo — if the figure is there.

## Hostile And Rival Pressure

The clock. @Sera is burning documents NOW. The hero has limited time to
recover the critical pieces. Every minute costs a page. The access code
payment is in the filing cabinet — but the cabinet is full, and finding the
right page takes precious seconds. Also: @Sera's guilt is a pressure of its
own. Breaking her too hard loses her cooperation. Not breaking her at all
loses evidence. There is a balance.

## Memory And Consequence Hooks

Record: whether the hero recovered the financial records, whether the hero
found the access code payment, whether the hero read Voss's note, whether the
hero looked out the window and saw @The Hobo.

## Do Not Do Here

- When the hero recovers the access code payment record — from the filing cabinet OR from @Sera Encrypted Laptop:
  - Entity: `@Greenhaven Police Department`
  - Type: state / thursday solved
  - Scope: @Greenhaven Police Department
  - Effect: Thursday's case is solved. The workday ends. The hero returns to @Greenhaven Police Department. The case board updates. @Metro Maintenance Depot — where @Dorn is running — will be the next lead in the morning.


Do not make @Sera a villain. She is grey — morally, professionally,
emotionally. She helped Voss without asking questions. She is burning evidence
to protect herself. But she is not evil. The hero's treatment of her is a
character test. Do not let the hero find Voss here. He is not here. The note
is the only trace of him.
